WiFionGo

Partner Terms of Engagement (ISPs & Venue Partners))

Issued by: Revolnet Technologies Private Limited

Last updated: August 1, 2025

1. Purpose

This document outlines the terms for ISPs, property owners, or venue managers ("Partner") who wish to offer public WiFi access through the WiFionGo platform. These terms govern the deployment, operation, and support of the solution.

2. Scope of Partnership

  • The Partner agrees to install, integrate, or provide access to WiFi infrastructure for WiFionGo deployment.
  • Revolnet will enable user onboarding, auto-authentication, and session management via its cloud-based platform.
  • The partnership may be structured as:
    • White-label deployment
    • Co-branded WiFionGo service.

3. Partner Responsibilities

  • Provide access to necessary network equipment (e.g., access points, routers, controllers).
  • Maintain basic uptime and electricity supply for the deployed hardware.
  • Comply with local telecom regulations (TRAI/DoT).
  • Do not share WiFionGo credentials or configurations with third parties without written approval.

4. Revolnet Responsibilities

  • Provide secure authentication and session management for WiFi users.
  • EOffer support and monitoring dashboards (if applicable).
  • Handle user registration and phone number authentication (as per Indian KYC standards).
  • Maintain necessary data logs as per applicable regulations.

5. Commercial Terms

  • Commercial terms will be finalized separately in a Letter of Agreement (LoA) or Memorandum of Understanding (MoU).
  • Pricing models can be subscription-based, revenue-sharing, or one-time setup + support fee.

6. Confidentiality

Both parties shall keep confidential all technical, operational, financial, and commercial details shared during or after the partnership, unless required by law.

7. Limitation of Liability

Revolnet is not responsible for:

  • Internet bandwidth or ISP connectivity issues.
  • Physical device malfunctions due to improper installation or power failure.
  • Loss of data due to third-party actions or force majeure.

8. Termination

  • Either party may terminate this engagement with 30 days' written notice.
  • Upon termination, access credentials, dashboards, and API integrations shall be disabled.

9. Dispute Resolution

  • In case of dispute, both parties agree to resolve amicably.
  • If unresolved, the matter will be referred to arbitration in Bangalore, Karnataka, India.
